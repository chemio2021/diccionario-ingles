Condign (kuhn-DYN) adjective

 

   Well-deserved, appropriate.

 

[From Middle English condigne, from Anglo French, from Latin condignus,

from com- (completely) + dignus (worthy). Ultimately from Indo-European

root dek- (to take, accept) that's the ancestor of other words such as

deign, dignity, discipline, doctor, decorate, and docile.]
