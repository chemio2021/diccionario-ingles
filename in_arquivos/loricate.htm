Loricate (LOR-i-kayt) adjective

 

   Covered with an armor, such as scales or bony plates on reptiles.

 

[From Latin loricatus, from lorica (protective covering, corselet),

from lorum (strap).]

 

  "The period movie stuffed with loricate soldiers, gladiators and emperors

   didn't need to defend itself against the slings and arrows of outrageous

   fortune Sunday, as `Gladiator' walked off with the Oscar for Best Picture

   at the 73rd Academy Awards."

   Kevin V. Johnson, Spizzerinctum Website, Mar 26, 2001.
