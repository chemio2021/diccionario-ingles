Don Juan

PRONUNCIATION:
(don wahn) 
 
MEANING:
noun: An obsessive womanizer.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
After Don Juan, a legendary 14th century Spanish nobleman, who devoted his life to seducing women. The story of Don Juan has been portrayed by many authors, including Moliere, Mozart, Byron, and Shaw. Earliest documented use: 1848.
 
USAGE:
"Oscar had always been a Don Juan ... but now that we were nearly 50, there was something desperate about his mania for conquests."
Leif Davidsen; Lime's Photograph; Vintage Books; 2002.
