Rasputin (ra-SPYOO-tin) noun

 

   A person who holds great but corrupting influence on another.

 

[After Rasputin, the nickname of Grigori Yefimovich Novykh (c.1871-1916),

a Siberian peasant. Rasputin gained entrance into the court of Russian Czar

Nikolai II and his wife, the Czarina Alexandra, by improving the condition

of their hemophiliac son. Over the years, Rasputin's influence over the

Czarina, and the court, increased tremendously. He was notorious for his

debauchery and was later assassinated by Russian noblemen.]
