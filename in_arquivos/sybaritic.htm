Sybaritic

PRONUNCIATION:
(sib-uh-RIT-ik) 
 
MEANING:
adjective: Devoted to or relating to luxury and pleasure.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
After Sybaris, an ancient Greek city in southern Italy noted for its wealth, and whose residents were notorious for their love of luxury. Earliest documented use: 1619.
 
USAGE:
“His father’s sybaritic lifestyle ... inspired in Mario a lifelong interest in worldly pleasures.”
Mario Reading (obituary); The Daily Telegraph (London, UK); Mar 29, 2017.
