Kleptomania

PRONUNCIATION:
(klep-tuh-MAY-nee-uh) 
 
MEANING:
noun: An obsessive urge to steal, driven by emotional disturbance rather than material need.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Greek klepto- (theft) + -mania (madness). Earliest documented use: 1830.
 
USAGE:
“College student Lydia Marie Cormaney almost made it out of a Gillette, Wyoming, Walmart with more than $2,000 worth of merchandise without paying for it. When police arrived, she was ready with a reason: She was doing research about kleptomania, which also explained the stockpile of stolen items in her dorm room. But, as she was enrolled in only a biology class at Gillette College, it was unclear what she planned to do with the results of her study.”
News of the Weird; Hartford Courant (Connecticut); Jul 13, 2017.
