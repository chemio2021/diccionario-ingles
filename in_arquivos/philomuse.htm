Philomuse

PRONUNCIATION:
(FIL-oh-myooz) 
 
MEANING:
noun: A poetry lover.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Greek philo- (love) + Muse (any of nine goddesses in Greek mythology who presided over arts and sciences). Earliest documented use: 1654.
