Dramatis personae

PRONUNCIATION:

(DRAM-uh-tis puhr-SO-nee)

MEANING:

noun:
1. The characters in a play or story.
2. The people involved in an event.

ETYMOLOGY:

From Latin dramatis personae (persons of the drama), from drama (play) + persona (mask, character in a play, person).

USAGE:
