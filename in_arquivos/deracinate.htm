Deracinate (di-RAS-uh-nayt) verb tr.

 

   1. To uproot.

   2. To displace someone or something from a native culture or environment.

 

[From French deraciner, from de- + racine (root), from Late Latin radicina,

from Latin radix (root), ultimately from Indo-European root wrad (root)

which is also the source of words, such as root, wort, licorice, radical,

radish, rutabaga, eradicate, and ramify.]
