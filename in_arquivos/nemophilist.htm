Nemophilist

PRONUNCIATION:
(ni/nuh-MOF-uh-list) 
 
MEANING:
noun: One who loves forests.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Greek nemos (grove, woods) + -philist (lover). Earliest documented use: 1860.
 
USAGE:
“It was sad that such an ardent nemophilist should be afraid in the forest.”
Edgar Rice Burroughs; Tarzan and the Castaways; Canaveral Press; 1965.
