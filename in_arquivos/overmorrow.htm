Overmorrow

PRONUNCIATION:
(oh-vuhr-MOR-oh) 
 
MEANING:
noun: The day after tomorrow.
adjective: Of or relating to the day after tomorrow.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From over (above) + morrow (tomorrow), from Old English morgen (morning). Earliest documented use: 1535. Also seehodiernal (relating to today), hesternal (relating to yesterday), and nudiustertian (relating to the day before yesterday).
 
USAGE:
"We can go not overmorrow, but on Thursday."
The Parliamentary Debates; H.M. Stationery Office; 1925.
