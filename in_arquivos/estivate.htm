Estivate/aestivate

PRONUNCIATION:
(ES-tuh-vayt) 
 
MEANING:
verb: To pass the summer in a dormant state.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Latin aestivare (to spend the summer). Earliest documented use: 1623.
 
USAGE:
“Unlike aquatic species, the turtles have the ability to travel upland and estivate for the remainder of the summer.”
Marta Yamamoto; Popular Berkeley Lake Reduced to a Puddle; The Mercury News (San Jose, California); Sep 17, 2015.
