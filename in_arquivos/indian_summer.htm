Indian summer (IN-dee-uhn SUM-uhr) noun

 

   1. A period of unseasonably warm weather in late autumn or early winter.

   2. A pleasant or flourishing period toward the end of something.

 

[Apparently from the fact that this weather phenomenon was first

noticed in areas inhabited by Native Americans (erroneously called

Indians), in the US.]

 

A related term is Saint Martin's summer, that is Indian summer

occurring in November. (from Saint Martin's Day, November 11).
