Proxemics

PRONUNCIATION:
(prok-SEE-miks) 
 
MEANING:
noun: The study of physical proximity between people, for example, typical space between two friends.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
Coined by the anthropologist Edward T. Hall (1914-2009). From proximity (nearness), from French proximité from Latin proximitas, from proximus (nearest), superlative of prope (near). Ultimately from the Indo-European root per- (forward, through), which also gave us paramount, prime, proton,prow, probity, German Frau (woman), and Hindi purana (old). Earliest documented use: 1963.
