Ducat (DUK-uht) noun

 

   1. An admission ticket.

   2. A piece of money.

   3. Any of various gold coins formerly used in some European countries.

 

[From Middle English, from Old French, from Old Italian ducato, from

Late Latin ductus, from duchy (so named because the word appeared on

some early ducats), from ducy (a territory ruled by a duke or a duchess).]
