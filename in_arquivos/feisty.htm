Feisty (FY-stee) adjective

 

   1. Spirited; full of courage, spunk, or energy.

 

   2. Touchy, irritable, or ill-tempered.

 

[From feist, variant of obsolete fist, short for fisting cur, a contemptuous

term for a dog, from fist, from Middle English fisten (to break wind). The

word fizzle is ultimately derived from the same source.]

 

  "The disputed territory - remote but fertile and said to contain gold - is

   not the real issue. More important is the fact that Ethiopia, big, ancient

   and proud, has its main access to the outside world controlled by small,

   new, feisty Eritrea."

   Africa: The New Princes Fall Out, The Economist (London), May 23, 1998.

 

  "Roxelane, it turns out, was a feisty character in a comic play that Haydn

   wrote some music for."

   Greg Sandow, Hi, I'm Wolfgang and I'll be Your Composer, The Wall Street

   Journal (New York), Aug 27, 2002.
