Endsville

PRONUNCIATION:
(ENDZ-vil) 
 
MEANING:
noun: 1. Something that is most excellent or the ultimate.
  2. Something that is most undesirable; the end.
adj.: 1. Most excellent.
  2. Most undesirable.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From end + French ville (city). Earliest documented use: 1954.

 "'To have peace in yourself - that must be the greatest thing in the

   world,' he said soberly. 'That is what I am looking for. To me that

   is the greatest thing in living. It is Endsville itself.'"

   Hal Boyle; La Rosa Hopes to Launch Own Television Program;

   Zanesville Signal (Ohio); Oct 19, 1954.

 

  "'Once they start involving the cattle grounds and building networks of

   roads it will be Endsville for the caribou,' he warns."

   Darcy Henton; Diamond Fever Gripping N.W.T. Amid Enthusiasm Comes Concern

   Over Environment, Land Claims; Toronto Star (Canada); Mar 31, 1996.
