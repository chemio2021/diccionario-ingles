Slipshod (SLIP-shod) adjective
 
   1. Careless; sloppy; shabby.
   2. Wearing loose shoes or slippers, especially those worn down at the heel.
 
[From slip (slide) + shod (wearing shoes), past and past participle of shoe.]
 
Today's word in Visual Thesaurus: http://visualthesaurus.com/?w1=slipshod
 
The literal sense of this word (worn at the heel), now considered archaic,
gave rise to the figurative sense (careless). The poet William Cowper used
it in its original sense when he wrote these words in his 1781 poem entitled
Truth:
 
  "The shivering urchin, bending as he goes,
   With slipshod heels ..."
