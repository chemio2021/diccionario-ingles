Immix (i-MIKS) verb tr.

   To mix; to blend.

[Back-formation of Middle English immixt, from Latin immixtus, past
participle of immiscere (to blend), from in- (intensive prefix) +
miscere (to mix). Ultimately from the Indo-European root meik- (to mix)
that's also the source of mix, miscellaneous, meddle, medley, melee,
promiscuous, and mustang.]
