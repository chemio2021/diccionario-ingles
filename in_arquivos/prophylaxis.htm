Prophylaxis (pro-fuh-LAK-sis, prof-) noun

   Prevention of or protective treatment for disease.

[New Latin, from Greek prophulaktikos, from prophulassein, to take
precautions against : pro-, before + phulassein, to protect (from phulax,
guard).]

   "I thought back to my own three years in Nigeria, and how different my
   health and indeed my life might have been if suddenly, in the middle of
   our stay, our supply of anti-malaria prophylaxis had been cut off."
   Meg Skinner, Worrying That the Bombs Might Target an African Friend,
   Capital Times (Madison, WI), Aug 31, 1998.
