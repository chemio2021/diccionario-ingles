Fogram or fogrum (FO-gruhm) noun

 

   A person with old-fashioned or overly conservative attitudes.

 

[Of uncertain origin.]

 

  "...so civil to all the old fograms, you would make one imagine you liked nobody so well."

   Frances Burney, Margaret Anne Doody, and Peter Sabor; Cecilia: Memoirs of

   an Heiress; Oxford Press; 1999.

 

  "Just as he had done making himself up, in came another old fogram of his

   acquaintance, by name the Count of Asumar. This genius made no secret

   of his grey locks; leant upon a stick, and seemed to plume himself on

   his venerable age instead of wishing to appear in the hey-day of his prime."

   A.R. LeSage; The Adventures of Gil Blas (translated from French by Tobias

   Smollett); George Routledge & Sons; 1912.
