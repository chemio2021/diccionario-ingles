Entente (ahn-TAHNT) noun

 

  1. A friendly understanding or agreement between two or more parties, governments, etc.

  2. The parties to such an agreement.

 

[From French entente (understanding), from Old French entente (intent),

past participle of entendre (to understand, intend), from Latin intendere,

from in- (toward) + tendere (to stretch). Other words derived from the

same Latin root are attend, extend, pretend, tense, and tender.]

 

The year 2004 marks the hundredth anniversary of the Entente Cordiale,

the agreement signed between the UK and France in 1904, ending centuries

of hostilities: http://www.entente-cordiale.org .
