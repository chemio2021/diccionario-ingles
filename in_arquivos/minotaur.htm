Minotaur (MIN-uh-tawr) noun

 

   Someone or something monstrous, especially one that devours.

 

[From Latin Minotaurus, from Greek Minotauros, from Minos (a king of Crete)

+ tauros (bull).]

 

In Greek mythology, Minotaur was a monster with a bull's head and a man's

body. He was confined in a labyrinth designed by Daedalus, and devoured

seven youths and seven maidens every year until Theseus killed him.
