Cyclopean (sy-kluh-PEE-uhn, si-KLOP-ee-uhn) adjective

 

   1. Of or suggestive of Cyclops; one-eyed.

   2. Huge.

   3. Formed with large, irregular stones closely fitted without the use of mortar.

 

[From Latin Cyclopeus, from Greek Kyklops (Cyclops), from kyklos (circle)

+ ops (eye). Cyclops were a race of savage one-eyed giants in Greek

mythology. They forged thunderbolts for Zeus in return for their freedom.

Cyclopean walls were attributed to them for their strength in building such

massive walls.]
