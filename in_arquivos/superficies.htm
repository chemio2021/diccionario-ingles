Superficies (soo-puhr-FISH-ee-eez) noun
 
Outer surface or appearance of something or someone.
 
[From Latin superficies, from super (over, above) + facies (form, face),
from facere (to make or do). Ultimately from the Indo-European root dhe-
(to set or put) that is also the source of do, deed, factory, fashion,
face, rectify, defeat, sacrifice, satisfy, Sanskrit sandhi (literally,
joining), Urdu purdah (literally, veil or curtain), and Russian duma
(council).]
