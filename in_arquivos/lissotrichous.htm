Lissotrichous (li-SO-tri-kuhs) adjective

 

   Having straight or smooth hair.

 

[From Greek lissós (smooth) + trich-, from thrix, (hair). Some cousins

of this word are cymotrichous (having wavy hair), trichotillomania (the

compulsion to pull out one's hair), and its end result atrichia (baldness).]

 

  "Madonna's Blonde Ambition tour notwithstanding, women really aspire to

   be lissotrichous brunettes, since sleekness and shine - the season's

   chief criteria - show much better on dark hair."

   Pamela Swanigan; Blondness: It's Probably Not the Real Thing;

   Vancouver Sun (Canada); Jun 16, 2001.
