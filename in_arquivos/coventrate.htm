Coventrate

PRONUNCIATION:
(KOV-uhn-trayt) 
 
MEANING:
verb tr.: To devastate, such as by heavy bombing.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
After Coventry, a city in central England, that was devastated in German bombing during WWII, Nov 14-15, 1940. The Germans coined the verb coventrieren (to coventrate) after the city to describe any heavy bombing, and the term was adopted in English as well. Earliest documented use: 1940. See also, blitzkrieg.
 
USAGE:
“Fighter and bomber commands were at the heart of the defensive and offensive strategy; Germany’s cities would be Coventrated.”
Paul Oestreicher; The Legacy of Dresden; The Guardian (London, UK); Mar 3, 2004.
