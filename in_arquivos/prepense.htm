Prepense (pri-PENS) adjective

   Planned; premeditated.

[From Anglo-Norman purpenser (to premeditate), from Latin pensare (to think).]

-Anu Garg (garg wordsmith.org)

  "As regards responsibility, the distinction between malice prepense and
   blameless or accidental commission of an act is not regarded."
   G.W.F. Hegel; Philosophy Of History: China; (translation: J. Sibree).
