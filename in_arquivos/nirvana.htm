Nirvana (nir-VAH-nuh) noun

 

   1. Freedom from the endless cycle of birth and death and related suffering.

 

   2. An idealized state or place free of pain, worries, etc.

 

[From Sanskrit nirvana (blowing out, extinguishing, extinction), from nis-

(out) + vati (it blows). Ultimately from Indo-European root we- (to blow)

that is also the source of wind, weather, ventilate, window and wing.]
