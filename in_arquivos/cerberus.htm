Cerberus (SUR-buh-ruhs) noun

 

   A powerful, hostile guard.

 

[From Latin, from Greek Kerberos.]

 

Cerberus (also Kerberos) was the three-headed dog that guarded the

entrance to Hades, the infernal region in classical mythology.

Ancient Greeks and Romans used to put a slice of cake in the hands

of their dead to help pacify Cerberus on the way. This custom gave

rise to the idiom "to give a sop to cerberus" meaning to give a

gift to quiet a troublesome person.

 

Cancerbero (from Spanish can: dog) is one of the Spanish terms for

a goalkeeper in fútbol (football). Kerberos is the name given to an

authentication protocol for computer networks.
