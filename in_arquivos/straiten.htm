Straiten

PRONUNCIATION:
(STRAYT-n) 
 
MEANING:
verb tr.:
1. To put into difficulties.
2. To limit or restrict.
3. To make narrow.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Old French estreit, from Latin strictus, past particle of stringere (to bind, draw tight). Ultimately from Indo-European root streig- (to stroke or press), which is also the source of strike, streak, strict, stress, and strain. Earliest documented use: 1552.
 
USAGE:
