Execrable (EK-si-kruh-buhl) adjective

 

   Detestable; wretched.

 

[From Middle English, from Latin execrabilis (accursed), from

execrari (to curse), from ex- + sacrare (to consecrate). Ultimately

from Indo-European root sak- (to sanctify) that is also the source

of other words such as saint, consecrate, and sacred.]
