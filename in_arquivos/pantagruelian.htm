Pantagruelian (pan-tuh-groo-EL-ee-uhn) adjective
 
1. Enormous.
 
2. Displaying extravagant and coarse humor.
 
[After Pantagruel, a giant king with an enormous appetite, depicted
in a series of novels by François Rabelais (c. 1490-1553).]
 
-Anu Garg (words at wordsmith.org)
 
"[Ilan Ros's] jowls are scarlet, and he's wearing a loose apron over his
Pantagruelian belly. He begins by gobbling up three slices of cold meat
in quick succession and then wipes his mouth on a paper napkin."
Yasmina Khadra (pen name of Mohammed Moulessehoul); The Attack
(translated by John Cullen); Doubleday; 2006.
