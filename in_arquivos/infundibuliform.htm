Infundibuliform

PRONUNCIATION:

(in-fuhn-DIB-yuh-luh-form)

MEANING:

adjective: Funnel-shaped.

ETYMOLOGY:

From Latin infundibulum (funnel), from infundere (to pour in), from fundere (to pour). Ultimately from the Indo-European root gheu (to pour) that is also the source of funnel, font, fuse, diffuse, gust, gush, and geyser.

USAGE:

"[Yossarian] might have remained in the hospital until doomsday had it not been for that patriotic Texan with his infundibuliform jowls and his lumply rumpleheaded, indestructible smile." Josph Heller; Catch-22; Simon & Schuster; 1961.
