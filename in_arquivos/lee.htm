Lee (lee) noun

   1. Shelter.

   2. The side (of a ship, for example) that's sheltered or away from
      the direction from which the wind blows.

adjective

   Of or pertaining to the side that's away from the wind.

[From Middle English, from Old English hleo (shelter).]

  "Sunlight spread in waves across eerily calm fields in the lee of the
   woods ..."
   Paul Evans, Wednesday Radio, The Guardian (London), Oct 30, 2002.

  "Eventually, the rain let up, and cabin-fevered, we stood in the lee of
   the dorm and watched the bowed trees and the sea-foam creeping up between
   the huts."
   Denise Fainberg, An Ill Wind in Paradise, The New York Times, Mar 3, 2002.
