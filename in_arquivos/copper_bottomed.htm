Copper-bottomed

PRONUNCIATION:
(kop-uhr-BOT-uhmd) 
 
MEANING:
adjective: Reliable, genuine, or trustworthy.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From the practice of covering a ship’s hull with copper (or alloy) to protect it from salt water and marine organisms. Earliest documented use: 1795. Don’t confuse this term with copperplate.
 
USAGE:
“In those circumstances, even copper-bottomed facts become contentious.”
Alex Massie; Breaking News: There Are Such Things as Facts; The Times (London, UK); Feb 22, 2017.
