Scrofulous (SKROF-yuh-luhs) adjective

 

   1. Of or pertaining to or affected with scrofula.

   2. Morally corrupt.

 

[From scrofula, a tuberculosis of the lymph glands, especially of the neck.

The word scrofula derives from Late Latin scrofulae, plural of scrofula,

diminutive of Latin scrofa (breeding sow), perhaps from the belief that

breeding sows were subject to the disease. In olden times it was believed

that a royal touch would cure the disease, which was also known as "king's

evil".]
