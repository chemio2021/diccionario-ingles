Enow (i-NOU) adjective, adverb

 

   Enough.

 

[From Middle English inow, from Old English genoge, plural of genog

(enough). Ultimately from Indo-European root nek- (to reach, attain)

that also gave us oncology (branch of medicine dealing with tumors),

from Greek oncos (mass, bulk).]

 

  "If we are mark'd to die, we are enow To do our country loss; and if to live,

   The fewer men the greater share of honour."

   William Shakespeare (1564-1616); King Henry V.
