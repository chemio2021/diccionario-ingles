Emprise (em-PRYZ) noun

 

   1. A chivalrous or adventurous enterprise

   2. Chivalrous daring or skill.

 

[From Middle English, from Middle French, from Old French, from emprendre

(to undertake), from Vulgar Latin imprendere, from Latin in- + prendere

(to seize).]

 

The following seeming unrelated words all derived the same Latin root and

involve the idea of seizing: prehensile, prison, prize, pry, apprehend,

comprehend, comprise, enterprise, pregnable, and surprise.
