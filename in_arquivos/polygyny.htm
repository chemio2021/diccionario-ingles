Polygyny

PRONUNCIATION:
(puh-LIH-juh-nee) 
 
MEANING:
noun: The practice of having two or more female partners.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Greek poly- (many) + -gyny (woman). Ultimately from the Indo-European root gwen- (woman), which also gave us quean, banshee, zenana, gynecology, and gynophobia (the fear of women). Earliest documented use: 1780.
 
NOTES:
A counterpart of this term is polyandry, the practice of having two or more male partners. The generic term is polygamy, having two or more partners.
