Shakespeare

PRONUNCIATION:
(SHAYK-speer) 
 
MEANING:
verb intr.:
1. To act in a play, movie, etc.
2. To attend a performance of Shakespeare’s plays.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
After the playwright William Shakespeare (1564-1616), who wrote more than three dozen plays. Earliest documented use: 1896.
 
NOTES
Not only has Shakespeare become a word in the English language, many of his characters have done so as well. Meet some of them here and here.
