Paterfamilias (pay-tuhr-fuh-MIL-ee-uhs) noun

 

   The male head of a family or a household; father figure.

 

[From Latin paterfamilias (father of the household), from pater (father) +

familias, from familia (household), from famulus (servant, slave).]

 

The feminine equivalent of the word is materfamilias.

Here's a quick question: What do the words "perpetrate" and "patriot"

have in common, other than the fact that both begin with the letter p?

The two words originated from the Latin root pater (father). When we

perpetrate something, we cause to bring it about or, in other words, we

father it. A patriot is a fellow country-man, one having the same

forefathers.

 

Other words derived from the same Latin root are expatriate (ex- +

patria, literally out of the father land), patron (literally, one who is

like father, a protector or advocate). Here are a few more: patriarch,

paternal, patrician, and patrimony. It's fascinating how the same root

gives birth to such a diverse range of words - not unlike children of a

father, where each child has much in common yet each has different

interests and temperament.

 

Ah, the joy of words! When we consider their derivations, we are, in

fact, tracing their lineage and drawing their genealogical chart,

identifying their fathers and forefathers.
