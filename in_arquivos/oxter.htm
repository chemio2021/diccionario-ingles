Oxter (OK-stuhr) noun

 

   The armpit.

 

[From Old English oxta. The Latin form is axilla. Both allude to the idea

of the axis around which the arm rotates.]

 

   "I got lumbered in the three-legged race with a guy so much shorter

   than me that he barely came up to my armpit. It would have been easier

   for me to have tucked him under my oxter and just carried him to the

   finish."

   Charles Jennings, Running Joke: Forget the Pupils, Sports Day is Hell

   for Parents, The Guardian (London), Jul 3, 2001.
