Passive-aggressive (PAS-iv uh-GRES-iv) adjective

 

   Relating to a personality disorder characterized by expression of aggressive behavior in a passive way, such as procrastination, stubbornness, or inefficiency.

 

[From Latin passivus (submissive) and aggredi (to attack).

The term was first introduced to describe uncooperative soldiers, in a 1945 US War Department technical bulletin. Passive-Aggressive Personality Disorder is no longer recognized as a proper diagnosis by the American Psychiatric Association.]
