Deign (dayn) verb tr. and intr.

 

   To do something reluctantly as if it's beneath one's dignity; to condescend.

 

[From Middle English deinen, from Old French deignier (to deem worthy),

from Latin dignare, a form of dignari, from dignus (worthy). Ultimately

from Indo-European root dek- (to take or accept). Other words from the

same root are decent, doctor, paradox, decorate, dignity, disdain,

indignant, and disciple.]
