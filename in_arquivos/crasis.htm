Crasis (KRAY-sis) noun

 

   1. Composition; constitution; blending.

   2. Contraction of two vowels into one long vowel or into a diphthong.

 

[From Greek krasis (mixture, blend), from kerannynai (to mix).]

 

  "However, I do not wish to condemn the Greek-Latin crasis. After all

   I have used it to mould my surname (Latro=thief [Latin], Nike=victory

   [Greek]) into a more palatable form (victory over the thieves) than

   might be inferred from its slanderous (for me) Latin stem!"

   Nicola Latronico; Making Sense; The Lancet (London, UK); Mar 30, 1996.
