Pointillage

PRONUNCIATION:
(pwan-tee-YAZH) 
 
MEANING:
noun: A style of painting in which small dots are applied to the canvas. Also known as pointillism.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From French pointiller (to paint small dots), from Latin punctum (point), from pungere (to prick). Earliest documented use: 1887.
 
USAGE:
“A dog’s footpads are filled with blood vessels, so he bled significantly during the night, at the same time making the beige carpet in Klaus’s spare bedroom into a wall-to-wall pointillage of bloody paw prints.”
Brad Steel; Mute; Graphos Books; 2005.
