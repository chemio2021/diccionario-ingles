October surprise (ok-TOH-buhr suhr-PRYZ) noun

 

   A last minute surprise, especially one orchestrated by a candidate to influence an election.

 

[The US presidential elections take place on the first Tuesday in November.

The idea of an October surprise stems from the belief that a significant

event taking place just before the election would influence the voters and

change the result. The term originated in the 1980 US presidential elections.

US embassy personnel were held hostage in Tehran, leading to speculation that

the incumbent president would secure their release just before the election,

in order to boost his prospects for re-election.]
