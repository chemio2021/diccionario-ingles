Mulligrubs (MUL-i-grubz) noun

 

   1. Grumpiness; colic; low spirits.

 

   2. An ill-tempered person.

 

[From mulliegrums, apparently from megrims (low spirits).]

 

-Anu Garg (gargATwordsmith.org)

 

  "I got the dismals. Not exactly the dumps or mulligrubs, more like moody

   and uncertain, as when you don't know what's going to happen next."

   Fred Schmidt; Middle East Thing Puts Chill on Festivities; San Antonio

   Express-News (Texas); Dec 30, 1990.
