Fain

PRONUNCIATION:
(fayn) 
 
MEANING:
adverb: 1. Willingly; gladly.
  2. Rather.
adjective: 1. Pleased.
  2. Obliged.
  3. Eager.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Old English faegen (glad). Earliest documented use: 888.
 
USAGE:
“Now I would fain work, but am unable to.”
Henrik Ibsen (Translation: Edmund Gosse & William Archer); Hedda Gabler; Scribner; 1909.
