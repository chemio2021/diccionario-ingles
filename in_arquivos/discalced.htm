Discalced

PRONUNCIATION:
(dis-KALST)
MEANING:
adjective: Without shoes.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Latin dis- (apart, away) + calceare (to fit with shoes), from calceus (shoe), from calx (heel). The word discalced is often used of members of religious orders who go barefoot or wear sandals.
 
USAGE:
"There were several pairs of shoes involved as well, which the otherwise discalced women had a hard time getting on and off."
Christine Temin; 'Straight Up!' Falls Down With Dance Premieres; The Boston Globe; Feb 11, 2005.
