Truffle

PRONUNCIATION:
(TRUHF-uhl, TROO-fuhl)) 
 
MEANING:
verb intr.: To search, rummage, dig up, etc.
verb tr.: To stuff or to intersperse with something.
noun: 1. Any of various edible fungi that grow underground.
  2. A soft, round candy made with chocolate, often coated with cocoa powder.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From French truffe, probably from Latin tuber (swelling). The verb intr. sense alludes to the search for underground truffles, traditionally with the help of pigs or dogs. The transitive verb is from the stuffing of truffles in something being cooked. Earliest documented use: noun 1591, verb 1868.
