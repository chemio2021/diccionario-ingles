Calefacient (cal-uh-FAY-shunt) noun

 

   A substance (e.g. mustard) that produces a sensation of warmth when applied to a part of the body.

 

adjective

 

   Producing warmth; heating.

 

[From Latin calefacient-, stem of calefaciens, present participle of

calefacere (to make warm), from calere (to be warm) + facere (to make).

Other (some hot, some not) words derived from the Latin root calere

are chafe, chauffeur (literally, a stoker) and nonchalant.]
