Hidalgo (hi-DAL-go) noun
 
A member of the lower nobility in Spain.
 
[From Spanish, contraction of hijo de algo (son of something). A similar
term of nobility in Portugal is fidalgo, from Portuguese filho de algo.]
 
-Anu Garg (words at wordsmith.org)
 
"[The play] ended in a massive attack by U.S. troops, who illustrated
egalitarian principles by slaughtering hidalgos, peons, and enslaved
Indians alike."
Michael Feingold; This Week's Hot New Plays; The Village Voice
(New York); Oct 2, 2007.
