Presentiment

PRONUNCIATION:
(pri-ZEN-tuh-ment) 
 
MEANING:
noun: A sense that something is going to happen, especially something bad.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From French pressentiment (premonition), from pressentir (to have a premonition), from Latin pre- (before) + sentire (to feel). Ultimately from the Indo-European root sent- (to head for or to go), that is also the source for send, scent, sense, sentence, assent, consent, andressentiment. Earliest documented use: 1663.
 
USAGE:
"That the reader has a presentiment of what will happen doesn't necessarily impoverish its affecting mystery."
Ten White Geese; The New Yorker; Mar 11, 2013.
